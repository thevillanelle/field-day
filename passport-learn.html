The Concept

The most interesting knowledge is never in a course. It's in the person next to you.

The quiz captures your skills and interests. The match finds someone whose gaps align with your strengths — and vice versa.

Exchange Formats

How to teach someone one thing.

Resource Drop
The Article / Video / Book
Find one thing that taught you the most about your skill. Send it with a 2-sentence note about why it clicked for you. The context is the lesson.
Voice Note
Two-Minute Explainer
Record a 2-minute voice note explaining one concept in your area. Teach it like you're talking to someone smart who just doesn't know this yet.
Written Lesson
The Starter Guide
Write a short "if I were starting this today, here's what I'd do" — the 3 things you wish you'd known. No jargon. Just what actually matters.
Screenshot Walkthrough
Show Your Process
Screenshot your screen or your work. Annotate it. Show them what your actual process looks like. Behind the scenes is more useful than the polished result.
Challenge
Give Them a Task
Instead of explaining — give them a beginner exercise in your skill. Something they can actually try. Report back what happened.
Conversation
The Q&A Postcard
Your match sends you 3 questions about your skill. You answer them honestly — including the "I don't know" ones. Questions are often better than lessons.
