Commons

How It Works

Commons connects colleagues across locations through four different ways to meet. Here's everything you need to know to get started.

1

Pick your Passport.

Commons has four ways to connect — Play, Meet, Make, and Learn. Each one is a different kind of match. Pick whichever sounds like you right now. You can do all four eventually, but start with one.

2

Take the quiz.

Each Passport has its own short quiz — six questions about how you connect, what you're into, and what you're looking for. This shapes who you're matched with. It takes about two minutes and you only do it once per Passport.

3

We find your match.

Our algorithm pairs you with someone from a different location whose vibe complements yours. You'll never be matched with someone from your own city — the distance is the whole point. Matches drop on Mondays and you'll hear via Slack.

4

Connect on your time.

Everything in Commons is async. No scheduling. No live calls. You go when it works for you — whether that's a Tuesday at midnight or a Sunday afternoon. Your match is doing the same thing on their side of the world.

5

Leave a postcard.

When you've done the thing — played the game, answered the prompt, made something, shared a lesson — send your match a postcard. A screenshot, a score, a message, a photo. Something small that says you showed up. That's your stamp.

What's a postcard?

A postcard is how you close the loop with your match. It's not a formal submission — it's just something small that says you showed up and the connection was real. Whatever format makes sense for what you did.

Common questions

Do I have to be online at the same time as my match? ›
No. Everything in Commons is async by design. You and your match will never need to schedule a call or be online at the same time. You go when it works for you — they do the same.
Will I be matched with someone from my own city? ›
Never. Cross-geo is a hard rule. If you're in Singapore, you'll be matched with someone in Raleigh, Cork, New York, or another location on the opposite side of the map. The distance is intentional — it's what makes the connection interesting.
How does matching actually work? ›
The quiz results feed an algorithm that pairs people based on complementary vibe types, overlapping interests, and timezone proximity — with a bonus for fresh pairings (you'll never be matched with the same person twice). Matches are globally optimal, not just locally good: the algorithm finds the best set of pairings across everyone registered, not just the best match for each individual.
When do matches drop? ›
Matches drop on Mondays. You'll get a message via Slack with your match's name, location, type, and your suggested activity or prompt. The play window stays open for a full week, so there's no pressure to move fast.
What if my match doesn't respond? ›
Reach out to the event organiser on Slack and they'll follow up. If your match genuinely can't participate that week, a replacement can usually be sorted. It happens — don't stress it.
Do I have to play the suggested game or use the suggested activity? ›
The suggestions are exactly that — suggestions. If you and your match agree on something different from the library, go for it. The only requirement is that it works async and both of you can participate independently.
Can I do more than one Passport at the same time? ›
Yes. Each Passport is its own separate quiz and match pool. You can register for Play and Meet simultaneously, for example. We'd recommend starting with one to get the feel for it — but there's no restriction.
Is there a prize for participating? ›
For Passport: Play events, yes — there are usually prize tracks for most activities completed (Activity Passport Champion), highest scores (High Score Heroes), and a random draw from all participants who submit (Raffle). Other Passports may have their own recognition. Check the active event details for specifics.
What's the People page for? ›
The People page is the full community directory. You can browse everyone in Commons, see what they're into right now, filter by location or vibe type, and click any skill pill to find people who share it. Click anyone's card to see their full profile. It's also where the connections map lives — a D3 force graph showing what skills and interests cluster across the community.
How do I update my profile? ›
Go to People, find your card, click it to open your profile, and use the Edit button. You can update your bio, skills, interests, and hobbies at any time. The "right now" fields — status, song stuck in your head, current book, show, and hyperfixation — show up on the ticker and on your card for everyone to see.
